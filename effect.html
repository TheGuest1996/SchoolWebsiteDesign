Data from Generation Survey:

Platforms with algorithms designed to maximise engagement, like Instagram (86%) and TikTok (72%), are the most widely used across all genders. These platforms leverage features like “reels” and short-form videos that provide quick bursts of dopamine—the brain’s "feel-good" chemical—typically through 15 to 30-second clips. This content is short enough to maintain user attention while still triggering dopamine release, creating a reward cycle that encourages prolonged use. Over time, this can lead to addictive behaviours, where users feel compelled to return to the platform to experience the same satisfaction.

In contrast, platforms with less algorithm-driven content, such as Discord (33%), Reddit (15%), and Twitch (14%), are generally less popular. These platforms rely more on user-driven interactions and community engagement rather than algorithmic content curation, making them less effective at maintaining constant user attention and, as a result, leading to lower overall usage.
