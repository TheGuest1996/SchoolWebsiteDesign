Social Media's Effect on Teen Well-being



Social media use among teenagers is widespread, with many spending hours scrolling through platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and X (formerly Twitter). Concerns have grown over the potential negative impacts of prolonged social media use on young people’s mental health and well-being. While some studies highlight the positive aspects of social media, such as fostering connections, others point to risks lke social stigma, anxiety, and low self-esteem. The data collected was from two different sources, one of which was from Generation Survey and the other was from Pew Research Center. The data gathered from Generation Survey was available through their website. It is a large survey conducted in 2024 to explore the impact of social media use on the well-being of young people that targeted 3,785 Year 10 students across 340 Australian schools. The data gathered from Pew Research Center was available online. The sample consisted of 1,316 U.S. teens aged 13 to 17, along with one parent from each teen’s household in 2022.

Limitations and Biases

Since the data relies on self-reported responses, certain limitations and biases must be acknowledged. Research (Boyle et al., 2022), has shown that many teenagers struggle to accurately recall which social media platforms they've used over a set period. Additionally, adolescents often overestimate their average daily screen time, leading to potential inaccuracies. Factors like the type of platform, gender, and the number of platforms a participant regularly uses can further influence self-reporting accuracy.Consequently, survey-based studies may be subject to biases that affect the validity of conclusions regarding social media’s impact on adolescent well-being


Conclusion

In conclusion, the data highlights clear trends in social media usage and its impact on adolescent well-being. Platforms with engagement- driven algorithms, like Instagram and TikTok, are the most widely used but are often linked to LS for certain groups, particularly female and non-binary users. In contrast, platforms like Discord and Reddit, while less popular, offer community-based spaces that may serve as safe environments for marginalised users, though they do not always correlate with higher LS.

Overall, the findings suggest that the design and nature of each platform significantly influence user well-being. While the data provides valuable insights into these trends, the conclusions should be considered within the context of the study’s methodology to fully understand social media’s impact on adolescent life satisfaction
